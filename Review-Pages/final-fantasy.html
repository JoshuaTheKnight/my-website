Final Fantasy 7 Review

Final Fantasy 7 is a vast game with compelling characters and an amazing story. It is worthy of all the praise it gets. It is my favorite game in one of my favorite series of all time. I first played this game back in 2018 after being introduced to the series through Final Fantasy 15.

Gameplay

The gameplay of Final Fantasy 7 revolves around Square Enix's Active Time Battle system, which is a turn-based system that ditches the traditional sense of taking turns to move and instead gives each character an action timer that allows them to move when the timer gauge fills up. This creates an atmosphere of urgency because if you take too long, an enemy can attack multiple times. I enjoy this system because it tests my strategy under pressure and my ability to respond quickly to catastrophes.

Another high point of the gameplay is the Materia system. Materia acts like the skills in the game and is slotted into equipment to grant skills and magical abilities. Some Materia can be paired to create new abilities, like imbuing weapons with magic or allowing magic to hit all enemies simultaneously. It is a very in-depth system that allows for limitless build-crafting potential.

Beyond combat and build crafting, exploration plays a big part in the gameplay loop. You are given a massive open world to explore. Exploration is required to maintain a competitive level, find essential items, and even find new characters to join your party. I often found myself entranced by this exploration, whether it be spending hours at the Golden Saucer Casino or raising Chocobos.

Sound and Music

Being a PlayStation 1 title, Square Enix was limited in what they could do for sound and music given the space requirement, but somehow, they managed to create one of my favorite game soundtracks of all time. Songs like “The Oppressed,” “From the Edge of Despair,” and, of course, “One Winged Angel” still remain as some of my favorite tracks in a video game.

Story

The main theme of Final Fantasy 7's story revolves around life and death. It focuses on preserving the life force of the planet, what it means to be alive, and dealing with grief. Those themes are handled beautifully and often left me in self-reflection throughout my playthrough. The protagonist, Cloud, seemed like a one-dimensional cool character initially. However, as his story unraveled, he morphed into a complex, tragic character that still found a way to persevere through everything that tried to bring him down. I could write pages about the rest of the cast, but to keep things concise, I will just say that every character had something interesting about them. The main antagonist, Sephiroth, still remains as my favorite villain in a video game ever.

Artstyle

Once again, being a PlayStation 1 game, the art style is quite literally rough around the edges. Despite this, there still is a lot of character within the art. The enemies are unique, the environment uses a mix of images and 3d objects to create shockingly detailed environments, and the Chocobos will always only bring the art style up.

FUN FACTOR

With an engaging combat system, vast build crafting potential, and a story that managed to keep me hooked for well over 40 hours, it is no surprise that Final Fantasy 7 has a very high fun factor. The only places where I can see improvement lie in the limitations of the hardware at the time of development. This is a game that I recommend for everyone to try at least once.

Overall Grade: A+
