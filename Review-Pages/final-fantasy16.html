Final Fantasy 16 Demo Review

While Final Fantasy 16 isn't fully out, there is a demo available, and I was so blown away by it that I wanted to mention it here.

Gameplay

The gameplay was coordinated by one of the greatest action combat directors in the industry, and it shows. It is flashy, responsive, and extremely fun. I was only given a glimpse of what it can be, but it left me excited for what I will be able to do later on.

My favorite thing about the gameplay is the Eikon battles which are just Godzilla-tier monster battles between the core summons of the Final Fantasy franchise. The whole experience is cutscene level of polish, and it is such an enjoyable experience.

Sound and Music

The music in this game is mainly grand orchestral music that perfectly portrays the situation's tone. The Eikon battle music is loud and intense, and the moment-to-moment tracks are calm and respond to the tone of the conversations going on. The sound design is equally as good and sells the intense action combat.

Story

The story of the game is my favorite part so far. It is a dark fantasy story akin to shows like Game of Thrones and books like The Witcher, and it finds a way to perfectly incorporate the Final Fantasy feel through this setting by making the core struggle about the scarcity of crystals which is the everyday power source of the entire continent. I get a distinct Attack on Titan feel through the Dominants, which are people who wield the power of the Eikons and can transform into them. The demo's ending left me speechless and shocked at how everything went down. I am eager to play the full game to continue.

Artstyle

The art style of this game is impressively realistic through the power of the PlayStation 5. What I love most about this art style are the special effects used for magic and the Eikon battles. It's so large and explosive and showcases the raw power of these kaiju-like monsters.

FUN FACTOR

This game checks most boxes of what I envision as a dream game. A dark medieval political thriller with flashy action combat and large-scale battles. If this game manages to keep the momentum set up in the prologue, it will easily be my favorite game of all time.

Overall Grade: A+
