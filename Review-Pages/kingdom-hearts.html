Kingdom Hearts 2 Review

It is a no-brainer that Kingdom Hearts would appeal to me as a kid. It mixed Final Fantasy and Disney to create a fun and silly world that taught the importance of friendship. Kingdom Hearts 2 took everything great about the original and bumped it into high gear.

Gameplay

The gameplay of Kingdom Hearts 2 is the best in the series. The action combat is almost at a fighting game level of polish and is easy to learn but hard to master. Most enemies can be defeated easily enough, but there are some secret bosses that will test everything you know about mechanics. My favorite boss fight in the game was the optional Sephiroth battle because it was extremely challenging. It took me many attempts, but finally beating him was the greatest feeling ever.

Sound and Music

Like most Square Enix titles, their focus on music leads to a masterfully made soundtrack. Dearly Beloved, Hollow Bastion, and Traverse Town are tracks that I listen to daily. I also enjoy the remixes of popular Disney tracks. The sound design is also great, with the menu navigation sounds being my favorite in any game.

Story

I'm not going to lie, the story is a jumbled and convoluted mess, but I find it to be charming. I don't think I would be nearly as entertained by the game if they switched their storytelling to a traditional format. It is so fun to see Sora, Donald, and Goofy fumble around different Disney worlds and pursue a large-scale threat. It is not a well-told story, but it is very fun nonetheless.

Artstyle

I appreciate the artstyle of Kingdom Hearts 2 a lot. It finds a way to take in a large amount of Disney IPs and form a style that aligns with all of them. I am also a huge fan of Keyblades; I think that is an awesome concept.

FUN FACTOR

With fantastic combat, a goofy story, and a charming artstyle, Kingdom Hearts 2 is one of the most fun games I have ever played. It holds a special place in my heart because of how influential it was on my childhood. I have nothing but praise for this game.

Overall Grade: A+
